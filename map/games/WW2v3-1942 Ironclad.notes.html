Introduction

This game is a variant of the World War II v3 1942 setup, using a new circular map with additional territories and reviewed sea zones, and adding some extra powers, units and rules. It contains 3 different scenarios, each incrementally adding content to the base version:

  • Herkules: reviewed map rules (canals and straits, mountain passes, half-territories, strategic islands, naval bases) and new minor powers (French, Commonwealth, Neutrals).
  • Ironclad: reviewed units roster including new units (mechanized infantry and tactical bombers) and additional game phases and actions (Reconnaisance phase, submerging rules, airborne attacks, etc.)
  • Fall Blau: reviewed economic and production rules (convoy routes, railway, multi-tiered production centers, changed SBR and repair rules, etc.)

Map Rules

The map is a (distorted) azimuthal equidistant projection about the North Pole and displays a lot of "immersion" details that are actually useless for the gameplay. To give players the option of painting the map their nation's color, some small territories and islands are artificially linked with playable territories even if they have no connections with them or are situated in an another sea zone. The map also features minor (dashed) borders that have no value in the game but show the political situation of the world in the spring of 1942. All this is a graphic choice on the part of the designer, who apologizes if it has a negative impact on the game's fluidity.

There are a total of 200 game spaces on the map: 130 land territories and 70 sea zones. General (v3) movement and attack rules apply, with some modifications and additions detailed below.

Production territories are the main territories of the game and have an income value ranging from 1 to 12.They are indicated in uppercase letters to differentiate them from flavour text and bordered by solid dark line.

2. Half-Territories (optional)

A few territories, identified by an asterisk on the map, are too small to have an income value. They are in fact considered as part of an adjacent bigger production perritory but separated as start for political reasons. As soon as both territories are controlled by the same alliance, they are considered as unified and cannot be attacked separately. In practical terms, this means that allied or enemy units can only stay or enter the prodution territory. The half-territory looses any game value and is auto-conquered together with its related production territory. This rule can be disabled in the game options.

3. Strategic Islands

There are 20 strategic islands shown on the map in circular insets. They have no production value but are essential for sea and air movements in the oceans. After all enemyâ€™s combat movements have been completed, a player can scramble up to 3 fighters and/or tactical bombers located on each strategic island to defend against attacks in the sea zones adjacent to them. These air units can be scrambled to help friendly units in adjacent sea zones that have come under attack. They can also be scrambled to resist amphibious assaults, whether or not the territory being assaulted is the strategic island or another territory adjacent to the same sea zone. They may defend against the enemy ships conducting the amphibious assault even if friendly ships are not present. The scramble rule can be disabled in the game options.

Note: Although it is represented in an inset due to its small size, the territory of Gibraltar is not considered as a strategic island. It is connected to Spain and sea zones 12 and 13 and doesn't give the option to scramble aircrafts.

5. Mountain Passes (optional with Neutrals)

Mountain passes allow units movement between otherwise not connected territories (through an impassable borders). Howerver, this specific movement is only permitted during Noncombat Move phase and only if the territories making up the two sides of the pass are controlled by the same alliance. It is allowed even if one or both territories have just been conquered during the preceding Combat phase. Finally, only infantry and aircrafts are allowed to move through a mountain pass, and air units should spend one additional movement point to cross this specific border. This rule is only in effect if Neutrals are in play.

7. Naval Bases (optional)

Naval bases are part of the map: they cannot be built, damaged or destroyed, but are captured together with the territory they belong to. Sea units beggining their movement from a sea zone serviced by a friendly naval base can move 2 spaces instead of 1. Bases only serve the sea zone they border and not all sea zones adjacent to the land territory they belong to. They are considered friendly if they were controlled by the player or an allied power at the start of the turn. This rule can be disabled in the game options to allow all sea units to move 2 spaces in all situations.
